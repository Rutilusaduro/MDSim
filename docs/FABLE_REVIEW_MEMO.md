# Fable 5 Review Memo — IndulgeCare Clinic Shore-Up Master Plan

**Reviewer:** Fable 5 (fiction-game quality)  
**Subject:** `docs/GAME_SHORE_UP_MASTER_PLAN.md`  
**Scope:** Does this plan serve the erotic fiction fantasy? Mechanics considered only where they gate the fantasy.

---

## 1. Verdict: **Revise**

Not Major Revise — the plan's diagnosis is correct and its P0 stack attacks the real feel-breakers. The existing craft foundation is genuinely strong: the warming-tier action copy in `visitClinical.js` ("Heavy gut. Heavier lids. Room for more.") and the clinical-voice profile quotes already hit the voice contract, and the doorway wedge scene proves the team can write a choice where every option is characterization. But the plan reads like it was synthesized by systems people who respect the fiction rather than by fiction people who understand systems. It ships the *machinery* of denial→acceptance without budgeting the *moments* of it, it misses the setting's own signature ritual entirely, and its phase ordering risks shipping visible promises before payable payoffs. The fixes are additions and reprioritizations, not teardowns.

---

## 2. What the plan gets right

- **The warming tier is the single most important item in the document, and the plan knows it.** The gap between `clinical_plus` ("Probably noise, but I wanted it on the record") and `complicit` ("Bill Gluttony Consult") is exactly where the denial→acceptance arc lives — the register where she has noticed and is deciding not to mind. Making it P0 #3 with a scrub of gorging vocabulary from early pools is the correct instinct: the clinical mask only titillates if it stays genuinely clean until it cracks.
- **Rung-crossing ceremonies at P0.** Thresholds create events; silent stage crossings are the genre's cardinal sin. Firing a ceremony scene with priority over random weeklies means the most anticipated content actually interrupts the loop instead of getting buried in a log tab.
- **Surfacing the framing ladder in the UI is anticipation made mechanical.** Ghost rows for locked gorging actions ("unlocks warming") are the design-test answer to "what am I working toward?" — the player sees the indulgent action behind glass and starts working the framing stat *as foreplay*. That is choice-driven erotica functioning as a game.
- **De-duplicating blob monologues and moving from checklist to mutually exclusive visit clusters.** Copy-paste reactions across sixteen characters are the fastest way to make a reader stop reading; and an all-actions-once checklist visit is a slot machine wearing a stethoscope. Forcing clinical-vs-indulgent path choices makes every visit a small betrayal decision, which is the fantasy.
- **Week 1 fix honors the slow-burn contract.** The early game is where small things deserve enormous attention; an empty week 1 breaks the promise before it's made. Correctly ranked #1.

---

## 3. What's missing for a premium erotic fiction game

- **The weigh-in ritual — the clinic's own signature beat — is absent as a system.** The prose-voice contract says numbers appear only at ritual moments, where they land like events. A PCP office *is a weigh-in delivery mechanism*. Every visit should contain a weigh-in beat keyed to framing tier — flat number and deflection at clinical, her watching the dial at clinical_plus, the number read like praise at warming, celebration at complicit.
- **Framing-tier crossings get UI chips; weight rungs get ceremonies; but the *psychological* crossing gets nothing.** The clinical→warming transition is "the moment she stops pretending" — the confession, the genre's best single scene. Each patient's crossing into warming (and again into complicit) deserves a hand-authored ceremony at least equal to a weight-rung event.
- **No refusals, no resistance, no her-led beats.** Every planned interaction is player-led. Nothing budgets for a patient declining the snack tray at low openness, for refusal arcs, or for complicit-tier inversion where *she* books the extra appointment and dares you to chart it.
- **Aftermath and callback are deferred to P1 when they're the payoff of the P0 scene expansion.** Shipping fifteen events with no settles and no echoes will feel like more content and read like less.
- **No player consent surfaces or heat calibration.** Content toggles at new game, heat cap, per-scene opt-in for explicit band, warm/charged/explicit labels on every scene in the catalog.

---

## 4. Phase ordering critique

The A→B→C→D sequence is horizontal layering; horizontal layers produce a bad emotional arc for anyone playing between phases:

- **Phase A ships the promise without the payoff.** Visible ladder with hollow top rung converts anticipation into disappointment.
- **Phase B ships ceremonies whose prose lives in Phase C.** Rung-crossing events go out in scaffolding form.

**Recommended:** Insert **Phase A′** — one patient archetype end-to-end (clinical intake, weigh-in at every tier, warming confession, one refusal, one her-led beat, rung ceremony, callbacks, epilogue) before scaling horizontally. Pair B and C so no system ships without prose in the same slice. Move **epilogue matrix from Phase D to Phase C**.

---

## 5. Top 5 plan amendments

1. **Add "Weigh-in ritual system" to P0** — per-visit beat keyed to framing tier × archetype; the number lands as an event.
2. **Promote framing-tier crossings to full ceremonies** — clinical→warming confession per archetype family; warming→complicit beat.
3. **Add "Refusals & her-led beats"** — low-openness declines; complicit patient-initiated visit beats.
4. **Make aftermath + callback acceptance criteria of P0 scene expansion** — every scene settles and sets a flag a future visit reads back.
5. **Player consent surfaces + heat-band audit (P1); epilogue matrix to Phase C.**

---

## 6. The pitch, after these fixes

You run a strip-mall clinic where every visit begins with a clipboard and a scale, and every patient believes the scale is on her side. It is — just not the way she thinks. IndulgeCare is a slow-burn management sim about the distance between what gets charted and what gets fed: you'll spend the early weeks keeping your language immaculate, watching a perfectionist log two pounds as "probably noise" while you write *continue current plan* in her file, and you'll spend the late weeks being asked — asked — for the snack menu before the chart even opens. Every patient is a separate seduction with her own moment of stopping pretending, every weigh-in is a small ceremony where a number lands like a touch, and the doorframes, waistbands, and waiting-room chairs keep score so the prose never has to. The game's real currency isn't money or action points; it's the gap between clinical and complicit, and the whole design is built to make you feel exactly when — patient by patient, pound by pound — that gap closes.
